For The Animals For The Planet For Your Health
  • We are conditioned to believe animal products are normal, necessary, and natural.
  • Many ideas and ideologies are imposed on us by our society, and in the case of animals, we are instructed to love some and consume others.
  • We have normalised behaviour that we would typically deem horrifying.
  • Every year trillion animals are killed in our consumer-led society – which ultimately means we as the consumer have the power to end this mass animal suffering and slaughter.
Read more from Ed Winter book “This Is Vegan Propaganda: (& Other Lies theMeat Industry Tells You)”.
Animal agriculture takes up 83% of all agricultural land on the planet, but it generates less than two-fifths of the protein and one fifth of the calories we consume (Poore and Nemecek 2018). Raising animals for meat, dairy, and eggs:
  • produces 14.5 percent of greenhouse gas emissions, which is more than the total emissions of the transportation industry. Read more from Gerber and Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations 2013.
  • Switching to a plant-based diet would also allow us to feed everyone on the earth while simultaneously freeing up 75% of agricultural land: which is the size of Australia, China, the EU, and the United States combined. Read more from Poore and Nemecek 2018.
  • Whole food plant based diet has numerous benefits
  • Help the gut microbiome - lower high cholesterol - high blood pressure – boost immune system – reduce risk of heart disease, stokes, type two diabetes
  • According to the Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics, a plant-based diet has been demonstrated to be healthful and nutritionally appropriate for all stages of life, including pregnancy and infancy.
  • The meat, dairy, and egg industries all claim that animal products offer important nutrients that we can't get anywhere else, but this is simply false.
